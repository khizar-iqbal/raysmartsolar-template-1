## 1. Net metering policy status (NEPRA)

### 1.1 The headline: net metering has ENDED for new connections — Pakistan is now on NET BILLING

- **Finding:** On **9–10 February 2026**, NEPRA notified the **NEPRA (Prosumer) Regulations, 2026** (also reported as "Net Metering Regulations 2026"), replacing the 2015 net-metering regulations. For **new** connections, the one-to-one unit exchange is abolished and replaced with **net billing**: units you import are billed at your full retail tariff (incl. taxes/surcharges); units you export are bought at the much lower **National Average Energy Purchase Price (NAEPP)**. Settlement is monthly in rupee terms; a net credit rolls to the next bill or is paid out quarterly by the DISCO.
- **Sources:**
  - https://tribune.com.pk/story/2591628/nepra-rolls-out-new-regulations-abolishing-net-metering
  - https://www.dawn.com/news/1972203
  - https://propakistani.pk/2026/02/09/solar-users-to-pay-full-electricity-unit-price-under-latest-net-metering-rule/
  - https://www.samaa.tv/2087345995-net-metering-regulations-2026-net-billing-system-introduced
  - https://www.pv-magazine.com/2025/12/18/pakistan-unveils-new-net-metering-rules-for-rooftop-pv/ (draft stage, Dec 2025)
- **As of:** notified 9 Feb 2026; in force as of July 2026.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.

### 1.2 What happened to the 2025 ECC "Rs 10/unit" decision

- **Finding:** On **13 March 2025**, the ECC (chaired by Finance Minister Aurangzeb) approved cutting the net-metering buyback rate from ~Rs 27 to **Rs 10/unit** and authorized NEPRA to revise it periodically. After public backlash, the **federal cabinet declined to ratify it in late March 2025** — PM Shehbaz Sharif and ministers opposed it and sent the policy back for review. **The Rs 10 decision was never implemented.** It resurfaced in different form as the February 2026 Prosumer Regulations (net billing at NAEPP).
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.dawn.com/news/1897670/ecc-approves-amendments-to-net-metering-regulations-cuts-buyback-rate-to-rs10unit (13 Mar 2025)
  - https://tribune.com.pk/story/2536675/change-in-solar-net-metering-policy-put-on-hold (Mar/Apr 2025)
  - https://www.arabnews.com/node/2593508/pakistan
- **As of:** March–April 2025 (historical, settled).
- **Confidence:** HIGH.

### 1.3 Current buyback/export rate for NEW connections (CONTESTED NUMBER — handle with care)

- **Finding:** Exports by new prosumers are paid at the **NAEPP**, which NEPRA revises periodically (revisions flow automatically into agreements). Reported values conflict across outlets:
  - **Rs 8.13/unit** — reported as the approved figure, a cut of Rs 17.19 from the prior Rs 25.32 (Samaa, Pakistan Observer, TechJuice).
  - **~Rs 11/unit** (and "Rs 11–13") — reported by Tribune, ProPakistani, Aaj TV, Dawn ("Rs 10–11"), CNC Electric.
  The divergence appears to be between the computed NAEPP at notification time vs. figures discussed in stakeholder consultations. **Treat the export rate as "roughly Rs 8–13/unit, floating, set by NEPRA."**
- **Existing consumers** (pre-9-Feb-2026 agreements) continue at their old terms — unit-for-unit offset, excess bought at the previously applicable rate (~**Rs 25.32–27/unit** reported) — until their current agreement expires (see 1.4).

### 1.4 Grandfathering of existing net-metering consumers (PM intervention → amendment)

- **Finding:** Days after the 9 Feb 2026 notification, PM Shehbaz Sharif intervened; NEPRA issued a **draft amendment on 16 Feb 2026** and finalized it (reported **1–3 April 2026**) with **retrospective effect from 9 Feb 2026**: existing net-metering consumers **keep their old billing (unit-for-unit exchange and old buyback) until their existing agreement/licence expires** (agreements ran 7 years under the 2015 regime). **Caveat:** any "material modification" — e.g., expanding panel/inverter capacity — forfeits the grandfathered terms and shifts the consumer to net billing. Pending applications **submitted before 8 Feb 2026** were ordered processed under the OLD rules (Power Minister directive), and on **24 May 2026** Power Minister Awais Leghari ordered DISCOs to clear the backlog of 1,355 stalled pre-Feb-8 applications by **1 June 2026**.

### 1.5 Current rules for new connections (capacity, ratios, contract, process, timelines)

- **Findings (NEPRA Prosumer Regulations 2026, as reported):**
  - **Capacity cap:** distributed generation facility max **1 MW**; system must not exceed the consumer's **sanctioned load** (the old regime allowed up to 1.5x sanctioned load; the new cap is 1.0x — reported as an effective ~50% reduction in allowable size). Minimum ~1 kW (as under old regime; not prominently re-stated in 2026 coverage — LOW confidence on the minimum).
  - **Transformer limit:** no new connections once solar on a distribution transformer reaches **80% of its rated capacity**.
  - **Studies:** systems **≥250 kW** require load-flow/technical studies before approval.
  - **Agreement term:** **5 years** (down from 7), renewable for further 5-year terms under then-prevailing rules.
  - **Process/timelines set in the regulations:** DISCO must acknowledge application within **5 working days**; complete technical review within **15 days**; install interconnection/meter within **15 days after payment**.
  - **Real-world timelines (2026):** 30–90 days application-to-commissioning typical nationally; LESCO practical range reported **75–105 days** amid its 2026 backlog; K-Electric ~4–6 weeks for a 10 kW residential system.

## 2. Electricity tariffs (residential + commercial)

**Structural note:** Pakistan uses a uniform national tariff across ex-WAPDA DISCOs (LESCO included), set via NEPRA determinations + GoP subsidy notifications; K-Electric differs slightly. Bills = slab energy charge + fixed charge + FCA (monthly fuel adjustment) + QTA (quarterly adjustment) + surcharges + Electricity Duty (1.5%) + GST (18%) + PTV fee (Rs 35) + income-tax items for some categories.

### 2.1 Residential slab rates (uniform/LESCO-applicable base tariff)

- **Finding — base tariff slabs (unprotected residential, sanctioned load <5 kW), reported as effective FY2025-26 and still shown as current July 2026:**

  | Slab (units/mo) | Base Rs/unit |
  |---|---|
  | Lifeline ≤50 | 3.95 |
  | Lifeline 51–100 | 7.74 |
  | Protected 1–100 | ~10.54 |
  | Protected 101–200 | ~13.01 (reported ~Rs 13–14) |
  | Unprotected 1–100 | 22.44 |
  | Unprotected 101–200 | 28.91 |
  | Unprotected 201–300 | 33.10 |
  | Unprotected 301–400 | 36.46 |
  | Unprotected 401–500 | 38.95 |
  | Unprotected 501–600 | 40.22 |
  | Unprotected 601–700 | 41.85 |
  | Unprotected >700 | 47.20–47.69 (sources conflict) |

  "Protected" = consumers whose usage stayed ≤200 units for 6 consecutive months. Fixed monthly charges apply by slab (reported ~Rs 200–1,000/month for mid-to-high residential slabs; sources conflict on exact schedule — LOW confidence on fixed charges).
- **Context:** NEPRA's FY2025-26 national average base tariff ≈ **Rs 31.59/unit** (down from Rs 32.73 in FY2024-25); some Jan 2026 reports cite a uniform average of ~Rs 33–34 incl. adjustments. A NEPRA-approved **surcharge of ~Rs 3.82/unit was applied March–June 2026** (IMF-linked); a QTA of ~Rs 0.35/unit applied Mar–May 2026 per other reports (conflicting — both may have coexisted for different periods).

### 2.2 Effective all-in Rs/unit at typical consumption levels (ESTIMATES — clearly label on site)

- **Method:** slab-wise cumulative energy charge + typical FCA/QTA (~Rs 1–4/unit range seen in 2026: e.g., Rs 1.64/unit FCA April 2026; Rs 3.82/unit surcharge Mar–Jun 2026) + ED 1.5% + GST 18% + fixed charges. One independently published anchor: **300 units ≈ Rs 10,913 total ≈ Rs 36.4/unit all-in** (pricedata.pk example, Jul 2026).
- **Estimated all-in effective cost (unprotected residential, mid-2026):**
  - **200 units/mo:** ~Rs 31–36/unit (bill ≈ Rs 6,200–7,200)
  - **300 units/mo:** ~Rs 35–40/unit (bill ≈ Rs 10,500–12,000) — anchored by published Rs 10,913 example
  - **500 units/mo:** ~Rs 40–47/unit (bill ≈ Rs 20,000–23,500)
  - **800 units/mo:** ~Rs 55–65/unit (bill ≈ Rs 44,000–52,000) — above 700 units the slab benefit is lost and effectively the whole consumption is billed at the top rate (~Rs 47.2–47.7 base) plus ~25–35% taxes/adjustments
  - **1,200 units/mo:** ~Rs 55–65/unit (bill ≈ Rs 66,000–78,000)
  These align with press statements that grid power costs consumers "Rs 37–55/unit excluding taxes" and "up to Rs 50–65/unit" at high slabs (Dawn/ProPakistani, Feb 2026).
- **Sources:** as in 2.1 plus https://www.dawn.com/news/1972203 and https://freetoolforge.org/guides/why-electricity-bill-high-pakistan-2026 ("actual energy is only 50–60% of a summer bill; the rest is taxes/surcharges").
- **As of:** mid-2026.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (published slab data is solid; the all-in figures are OUR computed estimates — label them "estimates" on the website).
- **Durable phrasing:** "At 500+ units a month, most unprotected households in Lahore pay Rs 40–65 per unit once every tax and adjustment lands on the bill. Send us a photo of your bill and we'll show you your true per-unit cost."

## 3. Solar system prices in PKR (installed, turnkey)

**Market note (important):** quotes in Lahore vary enormously by component tier (documented Tier-1 vs undocumented panels; branded vs generic inverter), roof structure (elevated frames add cost), and whether net-metering liaison is bundled. Published "price lists" span a 2x range for the same kW. Prices below are presented as source-attributed ranges. Panel prices ROSE ~May–June 2026 on budget-tax fears (~Rs 7,500–9,000 per panel), then the tax didn't materialize — expect softening. Treat everything here as a snapshot.

## 6. Load-shedding status 2025-26

- **Findings:**
  - **Spring 2026 (April) official plan:** scheduled cuts of ~**2.25 hours/day**, placed inside the **5pm–1am peak window**, across LESCO, MEPCO, IESCO, FESCO, GEPCO, PESCO; **K-Electric and HESCO exempt** from this national plan. Actual urban outages ran under 2 hours as of mid-April 2026. Cause: a ~**2,500 MW shortfall** (low hydro, reduced RLNG, costly furnace oil) — expected to ease May–June as hydro rises.
  - **AT&C-loss-based feeder shedding (structural, ongoing):** feeders with **>40% losses/theft** face ~**4–6 hours** of curtailment; this is Pakistan's long-standing "revenue-based load shedding." Urban low-loss feeders often face near-zero scheduled shedding outside crisis periods; **rural and high-loss areas bear 4–10+ hours**, with worst rural pockets reported at 10–20 hours in some accounts (upper end is LOW confidence / worst-case).
  - **Summer 2026:** analysts expected load-shedding pressure May–August as cooling demand peaks.
  - **The paradox to explain on the site:** Pakistan has surplus installed generation capacity on paper, but grid finances, fuel costs, and feeder-level theft mean outages persist as a *distribution/revenue* phenomenon — plus voltage sags in summer. Backup value of hybrid solar remains a real selling point, but DON'T claim "8-hour daily load shedding everywhere" — urban Lahore's scheduled shedding is modest; unscheduled/technical outages and high-loss-feeder cuts are the real pain.

### 8.2 Common solar scams/complaints in Pakistan (for trust content)

- **Finding — the recurring complaints (Pakistani market, 2025-26):**
  1. Fake/social-media-only companies taking advances and disappearing (no office, no accountability).
  2. "Free solar panels" / fake government subsidy pitches (no such residential program exists).
  3. Bait-and-switch pricing — low phone quote, then "extra wiring/structure/net-metering fees" after the visit.
  4. **B-grade, refurbished, or relabeled panels sold as documented Tier-1** (lower real wattage; no valid warranty). "Documented" vs "undocumented" panels is a Pakistan-specific trust issue.
  5. Wrong inverter for net metering (non-export or unapproved model → exported units never credited).
  6. Net-metering "delay trap" — paperwork deliberately delayed while system runs without export credit.
  7. Cheap DC cables/breakers/earthing → fire risk and losses within a year.
  8. Overselling system size beyond the sanctioned load or actual need (worse under 2026 net billing, where oversizing is financially punished — exports earn ~Rs 8–13 while imports cost Rs 40+).
  9. Fake warranty promises (verbal 10–25-year warranties with no manufacturer backing or local service center).
  10. Installer keeping monitoring-app admin access, leaving the customer blind to faults.

## Cross-cutting guidance for the website copy

1. **The single most important message change for 2026:** payback math no longer rests on selling to the grid. Under net billing (exports ~Rs 8–13 vs imports Rs 40–65 all-in), **self-consumption + right-sizing** is the pitch: "size to what you use in daylight; batteries for evenings; export only the leftovers."
2. **Payback claims:** published 2026 estimates put well-designed residential systems at **~3.5–4.5-year payback** (CNC guide) — still strong because grid tariffs are brutal; but avoid promising the 2-2.5-year paybacks of the net-metering era. Confidence: MEDIUM. Durable phrasing: "typical payback of 3–5 years depending on your daytime usage — we model yours from your actual bill."
3. **Never print without a date:** panel Rs/W, buyback rate, tariff slabs, load-shedding hours, bank markups. All five moved materially within the last 12 months.
4. **Safest evergreen numbers:** Lahore yield (~1,400–1,450 kWh/kWp/yr), Pakistan's import/boom scale (2024 = 17 GW), the AEDB→PPIB merger, scam patterns, and the structure (not rates) of net billing.
